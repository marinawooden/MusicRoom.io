MusicRoom

Collaboration creates innovation

Hello!

Welcome to MusicRoom! We're a music-sharing platform for college creatives looking to share their music and collaborate with other musicians in their area! We know how hard it can be to find other musicians and network. We aim to provide a user-friendly interface and platform to provoke collaboration- after all, collaboration creates innovation.

Why did we make this app?

There are many existing apps such as Spotify, Instagram, and SoundCloud, but there are not enough apps that are dedicated to music and connect musicians online. The app “Music Room” is for musicians, especially college students. The app provides a place for college students to share songs, form groups and interact with other college students musicians. The app is very simple and straightforward because we think that the app should work intuitively, so we used some common design and features that are seen in other social apps. The app enables users to search based on their interests, for example, if a UW student looks for a singer near his location, they can just search based on school. It totally makes sense because if they want to form a group, location matters sometimes. They could search based on skill and genre too; many singers have different tastes and skill. The app encourages users to share their snippets online because they want to share their songs and if someone likes their songs, they could have the opportunity to interact with other musicians; users can leave comments on each other. The app helps college students to be creative and innovative.

Our Mission

Current music-sharing social media platforms suffer from the oversaturation of posts. For example, 20% of Spotify’s catalog has no streams whatsoever (Eriksson 2019). This poses the question- how many songs are there that could potentially be much more successful, but aren’t because there’s too much competition from established artists? Additionally, current music streaming platforms focus more on broadcasting music rather than driving collaboration and building a community of musicians. How can we provide more exposure for niche and/or beginning artists? To address both of these issues, we thought that providing a platform for these artists who might have a hard time getting a foothold in the music industry would be helpful.

Our idea was also motivated by our own personal experiences. We are more than just students and developers- we have our own hobbies and one of those hobbies is creating music. However, as people who aren’t actively pursuing a career in music, or an education in a field directly related to music, we’ve found it hard to create the same network as we have in the fields we’re studying. Additionally, we’ve found that currently there aren’t many social networking platforms that specifically cater to connecting young creatives, and those that do are either much too specialized, and focus on a specific kind of musician, or have a poor interface design and are hard to use. So, our focus was to create a platform that young musicians would want to use, whose design was easy to use and aesthetically ambiguous, so as not to inadvertently prioritize a certain style of musician over another.
